# Private Beaches on 30A: What Happened and What to Do
This is one of the most important things to understand before planning a 30A trip, particularly if you're renting a property that isn't directly on the Gulf. The beach access situation along Scenic Highway 30A changed significantly in the years before 2020, and the change catches many first-time visitors by surprise.
## How It Started
Rosemary Beach and Seaside have always maintained private beach areas for residents and their guests — this was part of their original community design. In Seaside, visitors can still access the beach by renting chairs and umbrellas for the day, but cannot bring their own cooler or setup. This was the accepted model for years.
The broader conflict began when Walton County allowed commercial beach chair vendors to set up along the Gulf in front of private homes. Homeowners — including former Arkansas Governor Mike Huckabee, who became one of the most vocal advocates — objected to having commercial operations occupying the beach in front of their properties. The resulting legal and political battle resulted in large sections of previously public beach being effectively privatised. Seagrove Beach, Seacrest Beach, and even the Watercolor Natchez Beach Access — all of which were genuinely publicly accessible for decades — became restricted.
## The Practical Problem
If you're renting a property that isn't directly on the Gulf, the rental listing may describe "beach access" that is, in practice, limited to physical access to the water — not the right to set up chairs, umbrellas, or a cooler in the dry sand. The distinction matters enormously on a hot summer day. Verify exactly what "beach access" means for your specific rental before booking.
## Solutions
The most reliable solution is the state park system. [Grayton Beach State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/grayton-beach-state-park) and [Deer Lake State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/deer-lake-state-park) both offer genuine public beach access with in-and-out vehicle privileges, facilities, and ample space. Entry is under $10 per vehicle — a worthwhile investment for a full beach day with guaranteed space and no restrictions on your setup. Check the [Walton County Beach Access map](https://maps.google.com/?q=Walton+County+Beach+Access+Florida) for current publicly accessible points.
For the ongoing discussion among locals, the [SoWal forums](https://sowal.com/forum/threads/private-beaches-in-south-walton-customary-use-updates-sb1622.113172/) and [SoWal reporting](https://sowal.com/story/the-latest-on-beach-chair-umbrella-rentals-in-south-walton) are the most thorough sources.
