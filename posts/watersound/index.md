# Watersound: Private Community, Public Fun

Watersound Beach is a private gated community on Scenic Highway 30A — but don't let that put you off. Vacation rental properties within the community are available for booking, and staying there gives you access to the beach and community amenities. The architecture is clean and contemporary, the landscaping is immaculate, and the overall atmosphere is quieter and more residential than the more celebrated communities to the east.

## Watersound Origins

A newer addition to the Watersound story is Watersound Origins, a development by [St. Joe Company](https://stjoeexperiences.com/) on the north side of Highway 98. Properties here can offer more affordable pricing than Gulf-front rentals, with access to community amenities including a large community pool and recreational facilities. It's worth considering if budget is a constraint and you're comfortable with a short drive or bike ride to the beach.

## The Big Chill

The primary entertainment and dining destination in the Watersound area is [The Big Chill](https://thebigchill.com/) (formerly The Hub 30A) — a lively, family-oriented complex with multiple food venues, two bars, live music on a stage, shopping, and a 25-foot LED Jumbotron that becomes the centre of activity on sports weekends. Current food options include Duos Restaurant & Bar, Local Smoke BBQ, Louie's Una Pizza, and Nectar Coffee. It typically has parking available, which is no small thing on 30A, and the nightly activities make it a genuine community gathering point. The Big Chill opens early (coffee at 8am) and runs until 9pm.

## Getting Around

The Timpoochee Trail runs through the Watersound area, making it easy to cycle toward [Seagrove Beach](../seagrove-beach/index.html) and [Seaside](../seaside/index.html) to the east, or toward [Seacrest Beach](../seacrest-beach/index.html) and [Rosemary Beach](../rosemary-beach/index.html) to the west.
