# Scenic Highway 30A: Complete Guide to Florida's Emerald Coast

Scenic Highway 30A is a 26-mile two-lane road that runs along the Gulf of Mexico in South Walton, Florida — tucked between Panama City Beach to the east and Destin to the west. It is, for those who know it, one of the most extraordinary stretches of coastline in the American South: a string of carefully planned, architecturally distinct beach communities connected by a shared bike trail, all sitting on the edge of some of the most beautiful water in the world.

## The Timpoochee Trail

Running alongside 30A for nearly its entire length, the Timpoochee Trail is an 18.6-mile paved bike and pedestrian path that connects most of the communities on the highway. It winds through coastal dune lakes, live oak canopies, and beach access points — one of the great cycling routes in the Southeast. Most rental properties include bikes, and dedicated rental shops operate throughout the corridor.

## Accommodation

Hotels on 30A are rare by design. The Pearl in Rosemary Beach, The Lodge at Seagrove, and WaterColor Inn are among the handful of true hotels. Most visitors book vacation rental homes and cottages through VRBO, Airbnb, or local companies. This shapes the entire character of the place — 30A feels lived-in, not resort-ified.

## Getting Around

Parking is limited, particularly in Seaside and Rosemary Beach. Plan your trip around the bike trail and your own two feet. Golf carts are popular but not permitted in Rosemary Beach or Alys Beach. For longer distances, take US Highway 98, which runs parallel to 30A to the north.

## Why People Come

The beach. Always the beach. The sugar-white quartz sand and emerald-green water are genuinely unlike anything most visitors have seen before. Everything else — the food, the architecture, the music, the cycling — is context for spending time on one of the most beautiful coastlines in North America.
