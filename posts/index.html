The Timpoochee Trail

Running nearly the full length of 30A, the 18.6-mile Timpoochee Trail is a paved path for cyclists and pedestrians that winds through coastal dune lakes, live oak hammocks, and beach community after beach community. It's one of the best cycling routes in the South and the backbone of how locals and regular visitors navigate 30A without a car. Most vacation rentals include bicycles; Bamboo Bicycle Company in Rosemary Beach is one of several rental outfitters along the route.

Where to Stay

🏡 Hotels are rare on 30A — that's intentional

The few hotels include The Pearl in Rosemary Beach (Forbes Four-Star), WaterColor Inn, and The Lodge at Greenway Station in Seagrove. Most visitors book vacation rentals through VRBO, Airbnb, or local rental companies. This is part of what makes 30A feel residential rather than resort-like.

Getting Around

Parking is limited, especially in Seaside and Rosemary Beach. The best strategies: bike the Timpoochee Trail, rent a golf cart (note: not permitted in Rosemary Beach or Alys Beach), or plan to walk. For longer distances between towns, use US Highway 98 which runs parallel to 30A — it's faster and far less congested than the scenic road itself.

Things to Know Before You Go

  • Private beaches — Much of the 30A beachfront that was once public is now restricted. Know before you book.
  • Traffic and parking — 30A was not designed for the volume of cars it now carries.

Why Come Here?

The beach. The quartz-white sand and emerald water of the Emerald Coast are genuinely unlike most of what you've seen in Florida. No rocks, no shells underfoot, often no seaweed — just brilliant white sand and water so clear it looks like the Caribbean on a calm day. Everything else on 30A — the restaurants, the architecture, the cycling — is the frame around that picture.
