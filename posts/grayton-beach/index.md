# Grayton Beach: The Soul of 30A

*"Strange people, nice dogs."* That's the unofficial motto of Grayton Beach, and it tells you most of what you need to know. The oldest community on Scenic Highway 30A has resisted the polish and planning of its newer neighbours. It's scruffier, more laid-back, and more itself than anywhere else on the corridor — the kind of place where artists, long-time locals, and people who found it decades ago and never left form the backbone of the community.

## The Red Bar

[The Red Bar](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+Red+Bar+Grayton+Beach+FL) is a 30A legend. It burned down in 2019 — a moment of genuine grief for the 30A community — and was rebuilt with deliberate care to preserve every element of what made it special: the cramped, art-covered interior; the live music; the eclectic menu; the sense that something unrepeatable was happening here. It came back, and it came back right. Go for the music as much as the food, arrive early, and stay late.

## Chiringo

Just across from the Red Bar, [Chiringo](https://chiringograyton.com/) takes its name from the Spanish word for beach bar — appropriate for a restaurant that sits 200 feet from the Gulf of Mexico on 30A. Opened in 2016, it serves elevated beach food with a coastal freshness that complements its setting perfectly: simple, excellent, unpretentious. The outdoor seating fills quickly on summer evenings.

## Drive on the Beach

Grayton Beach is one of the few places on 30A where you can drive directly onto the beach with a permit. If you've ever wanted to park your truck at the water's edge and watch the sun go down from the tailgate, this is your place. Permits are available through Walton County.

## Western Lake

The beach at Grayton sits adjacent to Western Lake — a rare coastal dune lake ecosystem unique to South Walton. Occasionally the lake outflows to the Gulf, creating a shallow tidal connection where visitors swim, kayak, and paddleboard between the fresh and salt water. When it's flowing, it's a remarkable natural spectacle.

## Grayton Beach State Park

[Grayton Beach State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/grayton-beach-state-park) is one of the best things on all of 30A. The park features ancient, twisted live oak trees draped in Spanish moss, coastal dune lake ecosystems, and some of the most beautiful beach on the Gulf Coast — all accessible for under $10 per vehicle with true in-and-out access. Cabin rentals and camping are available for booking in advance. If the private beach situation elsewhere on 30A is frustrating you, Grayton State Park is the answer.
