Grayton Beach: The Soul of 30A

The oldest 30A community — motto 'strange people, nice dogs' — home to The Red Bar, Chiringo, and Grayton Beach State Park.

The oldest, most characterful community on 30A. Motto: "Strange people, nice dogs." Home to The Red Bar, Chiringo, and a magnificent state park.

The Red Bar

The Red Bar burned down in 2019 and was rebuilt with deliberate care to preserve everything that made it a legend — the cramped art-covered interior, live music, eclectic menu. It came back exactly right. Go for the music as much as the food. Arrive early and stay late.

Chiringo

Chiringo sits just across from the Red Bar, 200 feet from the Gulf. Named for the Spanish word for beach bar, it serves elevated coastal food with a freshness that matches its setting. Outdoor seating fills quickly in summer.

Drive on the Beach

One of the few places on Scenic Highway 30A where you can drive onto the beach with a permit (available through Walton County). Tailgate at the water's edge at sunset — a classic Gulf Coast experience.

Western Lake

Adjacent to the beach, Western Lake occasionally outflows to the Gulf, creating a tidal connection where visitors kayak and paddleboard between fresh and salt water — a remarkable natural feature unique to South Walton.

Grayton Beach State Park

🌿 The Best Public Beach on 30A

Grayton Beach State Park — ancient live oaks, coastal dune lakes, pristine beach. Under $10 per vehicle with in-and-out access. Cabin rentals and camping available. The answer to the private beach problem.
