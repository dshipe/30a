# Alys Beach: 30A's Most Striking Community

There is nothing else on 30A that looks like Alys Beach. The community, developed in the early 2000s, was built entirely in white — every structure rendered in stark, luminous Hartung white plaster, with thick-walled Moorish arches, covered walkways, and courtyard gardens behind solid garden walls. Walking through it feels genuinely otherworldly: like a small Andalusian village dropped onto the Gulf Coast. It is the newest and most expensive community on Scenic Highway 30A, and the architecture alone makes it worth a visit even if you're staying elsewhere.

## Caliza Pool & Restaurant

The centrepiece of Alys Beach is [Caliza](https://alysbeach.com/amenities/caliza/), a poolside restaurant and resort complex designed by the community's own town architects. The main 100-foot zero-entry pool is surrounded by sun loungers and the open-air dining room, which serves contemporary American cuisine — think grilled octopus, wood-fired pizzas, seasonal seafood — in a setting that feels genuinely Mediterranean. There's also a lap pool with underwater music and a shaded family pool. Caliza is accessible to visitors (not residents only), though it fills quickly in summer.

## Dining

[Fonville Press](https://maps.google.com/?q=Fonville+Press+Alys+Beach+FL) has been a local favourite since the earliest days of the community — a casual café and provisions shop that doubles as the social hub of the village, open for breakfast and lunch. [George's at Alys Beach](https://maps.google.com/?q=Georges+at+Alys+Beach+Florida) is the long-established dinner option, known for an approachable menu and a laid-back atmosphere that feels right at home in this pristine white community.

## Nature Trail

A winding nature trail threads through the green spaces and dune corridors of Alys Beach, offering a pleasant walking route through the community's carefully maintained landscape. It's short but scenic — a good morning or evening walk that shows a different side of the place than the commercial centre.

## Getting Around

Like [Rosemary Beach](../rosemary-beach/index.html) next door, golf carts are not permitted in Alys Beach. The community is entirely walkable and bikeable, and the Timpoochee Trail runs right along its edge. Rent bikes or bring your own — you won't need a car once you're here.
