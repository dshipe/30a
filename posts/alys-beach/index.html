Alys Beach: 30A's Most Striking Community

Alys Beach is the newest and most architecturally dramatic town on 30A — all-white Moorish architecture, the Caliza pool, and Gulf-front nature trails.

The newest and most architecturally dramatic town on 30A — all-white, Moorish-influenced, and unlike anything else on the Emerald Coast.

The Architecture

Every structure in Alys Beach is rendered in stark white plaster with thick Moorish arches, covered walkways, and courtyard gardens. Walking through it feels like a small Andalusian village dropped onto the Gulf Coast. It is the newest and most expensive community on Scenic Highway 30A, and the architecture alone makes it worth a visit.

Caliza Pool & Restaurant

The centrepiece of Alys Beach is Caliza — a 100-foot zero-entry pool surrounded by sun loungers and an open-air restaurant serving contemporary American cuisine: grilled octopus, wood-fired pizzas, seasonal seafood. There's also a 75-foot lap pool with underwater music. Accessible to visitors, though it fills quickly in summer.

Dining

Where to Eat

  • Fonville Press — Casual café and provisions shop; the social hub of the village. Breakfast and lunch.
  • George's at Alys Beach — Long-established dinner favourite with an approachable menu.
  • Caliza Restaurant — Poolside dining; contemporary American with Mediterranean influences.

Nature Trail

A winding nature trail threads through the dune corridors of Alys Beach — a pleasant morning or evening walk through the community's landscape. Short but worth it.

🚲 No Golf Carts

Like Rosemary Beach, golf carts are not permitted. The community is entirely walkable. The Timpoochee Trail runs along the edge for easy cycling to neighbouring towns.
