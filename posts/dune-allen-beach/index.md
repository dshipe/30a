# Dune Allen Beach: Gulf Place and Great Breakfasts

Dune Allen Beach sits at the far western end of Scenic Highway 30A, and its commercial centre — Gulf Place — has undergone significant transformation in recent years. The once-quiet area, known for its Artist's Market and small independent businesses, was acquired by the owners of Alvin's Island and repositioned as a livelier, beach-bar-oriented destination. Long-timers have mixed feelings about the change, but the area now has more energy and foot traffic than it did in its quieter incarnation, and the two best reasons to visit — Perfect Pig and Skunk Gulley — remain as good as ever.

## The Perfect Pig at Gulf Place

[The Perfect Pig](https://www.theperfectpig.com/) at Gulf Place is the location that many regular 30A visitors consider the best of the several Perfect Pig locations on the corridor. Open for breakfast and lunch, the menu centres on house-smoked meats — applewood-smoked bacon carved at the table, pulled pork sandwiches, a grilled cheese made with proper care — alongside classic breakfast plates and a gourmet market section. The morning queue is real, but moves steadily, and the breakfast here is the kind that sets the tone for a good day.

## Skunk Gulley

[Skunk Gulley](https://maps.google.com/?q=Skunk+Gulley+Gulf+Place+30A) occupies a prime deck position overlooking the Ed Walline public beach access and the Gulf of Mexico beyond. The food is casual and solid, but the views are the reason to come — a wide, unobstructed panorama of the emerald water and white sand that makes any meal better by several degrees. It's one of the better vantage points on 30A for watching the light change over the Gulf in the late afternoon.

## Gulf Place

The broader Gulf Place complex has shops, casual food vendors, and the general feel of a beach commercial hub in mid-transition. It's worth walking through, but approach it with current expectations — it's not the quiet Artist's Market it once was.
