# Blue Mountain Beach: Quiet, Scenic, Underrated

Blue Mountain Beach is named for the blue lupine wildflowers that once carpeted its high dunes — this stretch of 30A is one of the highest natural elevations on Florida's Gulf Coast, giving it a slightly different landscape feel to the flatter communities further east. It's one of the quieter stops on Scenic Highway 30A, without a dominant commercial centre or iconic landmark, but its two main dining destinations have built real reputations among regular 30A visitors.

## Red Fish Taco

[Red Fish Taco](https://redfishtaco.com/) at 2052 W County Hwy 30A is genuinely one of the most enjoyable casual dining experiences on 30A — and one of the rare spots that usually doesn't have a long wait, even in peak season. The tacos are fresh and well-constructed, the atmosphere is relaxed, and the layout allows for both indoor and outdoor dining. They also host live music events — check the calendar before your visit. It's the kind of place you end up returning to every trip because it's reliably good and never makes you work for it.

## Blue Mountain Creamery

[Blue Mountain Creamery](https://maps.google.com/?q=Blue+Mountain+Creamery+30A+Florida) is a long-time local favourite for ice cream — the kind of small-batch, handcrafted shop that puts supermarket ice cream to shame. The flavours rotate seasonally, the portions are generous, and the line tends to move faster than you'd expect. It's a standard afternoon stop for families cycling the Timpoochee Trail through this section of 30A.

## Plan Your Visit

Blue Mountain Beach is a good through-stop on a longer 30A cycling day, or a quiet base for visitors who prefer calm over activity. The beach access here is generally less crowded than the headline towns, and the elevation of the dunes gives it a slightly windier, more dramatic feel on a breezy day.
