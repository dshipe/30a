# Sandestin: Resorts, Outlets, and Baytowne Wharf

Sandestin takes its name from its geography — it sits between Santa Rosa Beach and Destin along the Gulf Coast, and the portmanteau has stuck. It operates on a different scale from the intimate communities of 30A proper: this is a large resort complex with its own internal road network, multiple hotels, a marina, and several distinct commercial areas catering to different tastes.

## Village of Baytowne Wharf

[Baytowne Wharf](https://www.baytownewharf.com/) is the entertainment centre of Sandestin — a waterfront village with dining, shopping, bars, and a nightly events programme that makes it one of the most active night-time destinations in the South Walton area. It's particularly good for families who want evening activity: live music, children's events, and a festive atmosphere that extends well into the night. The setting on Choctawhatchee Bay is attractive, and the walkable scale makes it easy to wander between venues.

## Grand Boulevard

[Grand Boulevard](https://grandboulevard.com/) is the upscale outdoor shopping and dining complex at the entrance to Sandestin — anchored by recognisable national brands and restaurants, with the polished aesthetic of a premium lifestyle centre. It's a practical stop for dining, grocery (a Whole Foods and Fresh Market are nearby), and shopping before heading deeper into 30A.

## Silver Sands Premium Outlets

[Silver Sands](https://www.premiumoutlets.com/outlet/silver-sands) is one of the largest outlet shopping centres in the Southeast — over 100 stores of designer and premium brands. Rainy day insurance, shopping therapy, or a genuine destination for bargain hunters. It fills up on weekends.

## The Whale's Tail and Pompano Joe's

[The Whale's Tail](https://30a.com/locations/whales-tail/) at Seascape Resort is a direct-on-the-beach bar and grill with a live webcam — one of the great casual beach bars in the area. [Pompano Joe's](https://maps.google.com/?q=Pompano+Joes+Miramar+Beach+FL) is the original beachfront seafood institution for the area — a Florida Panhandle classic for decades.
