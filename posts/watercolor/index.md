# WaterColor: Kayaks, Trails, and The Truman Show

WaterColor is one of the most complete communities on 30A — a place where you could genuinely spend an entire week without running out of things to do. Built around Western Lake, a coastal dune lake that connects to the Gulf of Mexico, the community offers kayaking, paddleboarding, walking trails, beach access, a luxury hotel, and two very good restaurants. It also has a film-history footnote that delights anyone who grew up in the 1990s.

## Western Lake and the Boathouse

The [WaterColor Boathouse](https://www.watercolorresort.com/) on Western Lake is the place to rent kayaks and paddleboards for exploring one of Florida's distinctive coastal dune lakes. These rare ecosystems — found almost exclusively in South Walton — cycle between fresh and salt water as they connect to and disconnect from the Gulf. Paddling Western Lake, particularly in the early morning, is one of the quieter, more beautiful things you can do on 30A. The bridge across the lake is a scenic highlight — worth walking or cycling across for the views in both directions.

## Walking Trails

Cerulean Park and the surrounding trail network wind through WaterColor's green spaces and dune corridors. The trail to the Western Lake bridge is the headline walk — flat, scenic, and easily accomplished with children. Early morning walks here, when the light is low and the lake is still, are particularly good.

## WaterColor Inn

[WaterColor Inn](https://www.watercolorresort.com/) is one of the handful of true hotels on 30A — a carefully designed resort property with direct Gulf access and a Beach Club that's among the most appealing hotel amenities on the corridor. Staying here also gives access to Camp WaterColor, a children's activity centre with pools and a lazy river. If budget allows, it's one of the most complete resort experiences on 30A.

## Dining

[Fish Out of Water](https://dinefish30a.com/) is a long-time favourite — one of the few large restaurants on 30A with Gulf of Mexico views, serving lunch, dinner, and weekend brunch. The menu covers Gulf seafood well, the outdoor deck is excellent, and live music runs seasonally. [Wine World](https://maps.google.com/?q=Wine+World+WaterColor+30A) offers a sophisticated outdoor seating option in WaterColor's small commercial centre — not on the beach, but charming and relaxed.

## The Truman Show

WaterColor and neighbouring [Seaside](../seaside/index.html) were used as filming locations for the fictional town of Seahaven in Peter Weir's 1998 film *The Truman Show*. The house used as Truman's home is at [31 Natchez Street, Santa Rosa Beach](https://maps.google.com/?q=31+Natchez+St,+Santa+Rosa+Beach,+FL+32459) — a quick detour for film fans.
