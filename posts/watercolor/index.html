WaterColor: Kayaks, Trails & The Truman Show

Western Lake kayaking, walking trails, WaterColor Inn resort, Fish Out of Water restaurant, and a Truman Show connection.

Western Lake kayaking, walking trails, a luxury inn with Gulf access, and a film-history connection to The Truman Show.

Western Lake & the Boathouse

Rent kayaks and paddleboards from the WaterColor Boathouse to explore Western Lake — a rare coastal dune lake that cycles between fresh and saltwater. Early morning paddling here is one of the most peaceful experiences on 30A Florida. Don't miss the bridge across the lake for views in both directions.

Walking Trails

Cerulean Park and the trail network wind through WaterColor's green spaces. The Western Lake bridge walk is the headline route — flat, scenic, suitable for kids. Morning light on the lake is exceptional.

WaterColor Inn

WaterColor Inn is one of the few true hotels on Scenic Highway 30A, with Gulf access, a Beach Club, and Camp WaterColor (pools + lazy river for families). One of the most complete resort experiences on the corridor.

Dining

Where to Eat

  • Fish Out of Water — Gulf views, lunch/dinner/weekend brunch, live music seasonally. One of 30A's best.
  • Wine World — Sophisticated outdoor seating; great wine selection; relaxed atmosphere.

🎬 Truman Show Filming Location

WaterColor and Seaside were Seahaven in the 1998 film. The Truman house is at 31 Natchez Street.
