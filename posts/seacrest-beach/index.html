Seacrest Beach: Hidden Gems and Free Parking

Seacrest Beach spans two areas of 30A — including the best free public parking for Rosemary Beach and the legendary beach-only Crabby Steve's.

Practical, underrated, and home to one of 30A's great hidden-gem beach bars — Seacrest Beach rewards those who explore.

Free Parking for Rosemary Beach

The lot behind Peddler's Pavilion in Seacrest Beach is the best free public parking option for visitors to Rosemary Beach. It's a ~10-minute walk to Rosemary's town centre — a worthwhile trade-off on a corridor where parking is scarce and expensive.

Crabby Steve's

Crabby Steve's (10254 E County Hwy 30A) is only accessible from the beach — walk along the shoreline to reach it. Inside a gated community, it serves seafood and cold drinks with your feet practically in the sand. One of the most authentic beach bars on all of 30A Florida. The inconvenient access is what keeps it from being overwhelmed.

Two Areas to Know

📍 Seacrest Beach — Two Sections

  • East section (between Rosemary & Alys): best free parking, Peddler's Pavilion dining, Crabby Steve's
  • West section (between Alys & WaterColor): quieter, potentially more affordable rentals, good cycling access

The western section can be a smart base if you want access to both Seaside and Rosemary Beach by bike without paying beachfront prices.
