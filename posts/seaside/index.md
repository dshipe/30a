# Seaside: The Heart of 30A

Seaside is where 30A began, in the sense that it became the blueprint for everything that followed. This planned community, developed in the early 1980s, pioneered New Urbanism — the idea that you could design a town from scratch to be walkable, human-scaled, and genuinely liveable. It worked. Seaside is one of the most visited destinations on Florida's Gulf Coast, and the central Smolian Circle is the axis around which 30A social life rotates.

## Parking

A practical note first: since 2018, parking on or around Smolian Circle is no longer free. The paid lot at Quincy Circle (behind Smolian) charges approximately $10 and operates a strict no in-and-out policy — once you've parked, you're committed to staying. Arrive with that in mind. If you're staying nearby, or arrive by bike on the Timpoochee Trail, you sidestep the problem entirely. The [Grayton Beach shuttle](../grayton-beach/index.html) is another option in busy season.

## Airstream Row

Airstream Row is Seaside's defining food experience — a line of vintage Airstream trailers parked in a row, each serving something different. The one you must not miss is [Crêpes de Soleil](https://maps.google.com/?q=Crepes+de+Soleil+Seaside+Florida), known for the FFrose — a fan-favourite sweet crêpe combination. There's also BBQ, gyros, grilled cheese, hot dogs, and ice cream. This is casual, outdoor, mid-day eating at its best, and the atmosphere around Airstream Row on a sunny afternoon — families, frisbees, the smell of something good — is quintessential Seaside.

## Restaurants

[Great Southern Café](https://maps.google.com/?q=Great+Southern+Cafe+Seaside+FL) serves the kind of breakfast that sticks with you. The vanilla pancakes are the legendary item — fluffy, sweet, slightly crisp at the edges — the kind of dish people mention ten years after eating it. Shrimp and grits are equally celebrated. Get here early on weekends. [Bud & Alley's](https://budandalleys.com/) is a Seaside institution with an upstairs rooftop bar that offers a direct Gulf of Mexico view. Sunset here with a cold drink is one of the great simple pleasures on 30A. [Pickles](https://maps.google.com/?q=Pickles+Seaside+Florida) handles the casual burger brief — reliably good, exactly what you want after a morning on the beach.

## Shops and Culture

[Sundog Books](https://maps.google.com/?q=Sundog+Books+Seaside+FL) is the cultural anchor of Seaside — an independent bookshop with a curated selection, vinyl records, and the kind of atmosphere that makes you want to stay longer. [Duckies](https://maps.google.com/?q=Duckies+Seaside+Florida) sells children's toys, and its signature item — LED toy helicopters — can be seen flying over the central grass all day. [Amavida Coffee](https://amavida.com/) is the 30A coffee standard: excellent espresso, great atmosphere, reliably busy.

## The Truman Show

Seaside was the filming location for the fictional town of Seahaven in *The Truman Show* (1998). The house used as Truman's home is at [31 Natchez Street, Santa Rosa Beach](https://maps.google.com/?q=31+Natchez+St,+Santa+Rosa+Beach,+FL+32459). Film fans make a point of stopping by.
