Seaside: The Heart of 30A

Smolian Circle, Airstream Row, Bud & Alley's rooftop sunset bar, Sundog Books — the cultural heart of Scenic Highway 30A.

The cultural heart of 30A — Smolian Circle, Airstream Row, Sundog Books, Bud & Alley's rooftop, and one of the best sunsets on the Gulf.

Parking First

⚠️ Paid Parking Since 2018

Quincy Circle (behind Smolian) charges ~$10 with a no in-and-out policy. Arrive by bike on the Timpoochee Trail or use the Grayton Beach shuttle to avoid the hassle.

Airstream Row

A line of vintage Airstream trailers each serving something different — BBQ, crepes, gyros, grilled cheese, hot dogs, ice cream. The must-visit is Crêpes de Soleil for the FFrose crêpe. The atmosphere here on a sunny afternoon is quintessential Seaside 30A.

Restaurants

Where to Eat

  • Great Southern Café — Legendary vanilla pancakes and shrimp & grits. Arrive early on weekends.
  • Bud & Alley's — Rooftop bar with direct Gulf views. The best sunset spot in Seaside.
  • Pickles — Reliable burgers and casual eats.
  • Amavida Coffee — The 30A coffee standard. Excellent espresso.

Shops

Worth Browsing

  • Sundog Books — Curated books, vinyl records, 30A landmark.
  • Duckies — Children's toys; LED helicopters fly over the central green all day.

🎬 The Truman Show

Seaside was the filming location for Seahaven in the 1998 film. The Truman house is at 31 Natchez Street.
