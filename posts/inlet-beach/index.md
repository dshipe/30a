# Inlet Beach: 30A's Affordable Gateway

Inlet Beach sits at the far eastern end of Scenic Highway 30A, right next to Rosemary Beach, and it's one of the most practical choices on the entire corridor for visitors who want easy access to the area's best destinations without paying Rosemary Beach prices. Many of the rental properties here are on the north side of Highway 98 rather than directly on the Gulf — a meaningful difference in cost that can make the difference between affording a week and affording two.

## Getting to the Beach

The clever solution to the highway-crossing problem is a pedestrian and cycling tunnel that runs under US Highway 98, connecting the north-side rental properties directly to the Gulf. It's the kind of small infrastructure detail that makes a real difference to families traveling with kids and bikes — no light to wait for, no anxious crossing of a busy four-lane road. Walk or ride through, and you're on the beach side in under a minute.

## Dining

Inlet Beach punches above its quiet reputation when it comes to eating. [Cuvée 30A](https://www.opentable.com/r/cuvee-30a-inlet-beach) is led by FRLA Restaurateur of the Year Chef Tim Creehan — an award-winning casual fine dining experience with an extensive wine list that's genuinely one of the better restaurants on the entire 30A corridor. The atmosphere is elegant but not stiff, and it's become a destination for special occasions among regular 30A visitors.

[The Donut Hole](https://30a.com/locations/donut-hole/) at 12983 Hwy 98 East is one of 30A's great institutions — a bakery and diner that opens at 6am and runs until 10pm. The key lime pie is the celebrated item, but the breakfast menu is the real reason people line up on Sunday mornings. Expect to wait in season; it moves faster than you'd think and is absolutely worth it.

## Kids' Playground

Families staying near Inlet Beach should know about the well-equipped playground at [19 Windward Lane](https://maps.google.com/?q=19+Windward+Ln,+Inlet+Beach,+FL+32461) — a genuinely good outdoor play area popular with children staying in the neighborhood. In a part of the world where the default answer to "what do the kids do?" is "go to the beach," it's a welcome alternative for an afternoon when the sun is too strong or the little ones need a change of scene.

## Plan Your Visit

Inlet Beach is best approached as a base camp rather than a destination. Stay here, save money, cycle the Timpoochee Trail west into [Rosemary Beach](../rosemary-beach/index.html) for dinner, and east toward [Alys Beach](../alys-beach/index.html) for a morning walk. You get all the access with less of the premium.
