Traffic and Parking on 30A: How to Get Around

30A was not built for current traffic. Here's how parking works in Seaside and Rosemary Beach, and the practical strategies for getting around.

30A was designed for a quiet scenic road, not its current traffic volume. Here's how parking actually works and how to avoid the worst of it.

⚠️ For Anything More Than 1-2 Miles

Use US Highway 98, which runs parallel to 30A to the north. It's significantly faster and less congested than the scenic road during peak season.

Seaside Parking

Quincy Circle (behind Smolian) charges ~$10 with a no in-and-out policy since 2018. Alternatives: Timpoochee Trail by bike, Grayton Beach shuttle (seasonal), or staying within walking distance.

Rosemary Beach Parking

Best free option: behind Peddler's Pavilion in Seacrest Beach. ~10-minute walk to Rosemary's town centre. Free, usually available.

Getting Around Without Moving a Car

🚲 The Smart Approach

  • Timpoochee Trail — 18.6 miles connecting most communities
  • Bikes — included with most vacation rentals
  • Golf carts — popular but not permitted in Rosemary Beach or Alys Beach
  • Walk — communities are designed for pedestrians; many visitors go days without moving a car

⚠️ Pedestrian Safety

The informal norm of cars stopping for pedestrians has eroded. Exercise caution at crosswalks — including marked, illuminated crossings. Don't assume traffic will stop.
