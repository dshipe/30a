# Traffic and Parking on 30A: How to Actually Get Around
30A was designed as a quiet scenic road through a collection of small beach communities. It was not designed to handle the volume of car traffic it now receives in season, and the parking situation in the most popular towns — particularly Seaside and Rosemary Beach — reflects that mismatch acutely.
## The Basic Reality
During peak season (Memorial Day through Labor Day, plus spring break), 30A moves slowly. The two-lane road was never intended as a thoroughfare, and the combination of pedestrian crossings, cyclists, golf carts, and gawking drivers creates significant delays. If you need to travel more than a mile or two on the main highway during peak hours, use US Highway 98 to the north — it's faster and considerably less congested than 30A itself.
## Parking in Seaside
Seaside is the most acute parking challenge on 30A. Since 2018, parking on and around Smolian Circle is no longer free — Quincy Circle (behind Smolian) charges approximately $10. Critically, there is a strict no in-and-out policy: once you pay and park, you're committed to staying. Plan accordingly. Even paid parking can be genuinely difficult to find during peak periods. The practical alternatives: arrive by bike on the Timpoochee Trail, use the Grayton Beach shuttle (operates in season), or stay close enough to walk.
## Parking for Rosemary Beach
The best free public parking for Rosemary Beach is behind Peddler's Pavilion in Seacrest Beach. It involves a walk of approximately ten minutes to Rosemary's town centre, but it's free, usually available, and avoids the circular search for Rosemary Beach's limited paid spots.
## Getting Around Without a Car
The most reliable strategy on 30A is not having to move a car at all:
- **Timpoochee Trail** — 18.6 miles of paved cycling path connecting most communities
- **Bikes** — included with most rentals; the trail is flat and accessible
- **Golf carts** — popular but not permitted in Rosemary Beach or Alys Beach
- **Walking** — the communities are genuinely designed for pedestrian access; if you're staying in one town, you may not need a car for days

## Pedestrian Caution
A practical safety note: there was historically an informal understanding among 30A drivers that cars would stop for pedestrians crossing the road, even at unmarked points. That norm has eroded significantly. Exercise caution at crossings, including at marked and illuminated pedestrian crosswalks. Don't assume traffic will stop.
