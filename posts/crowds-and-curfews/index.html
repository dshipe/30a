Crowds, Curfews, and When to Visit 30A

30A changed after 2020. Spring break curfews, peak season crowds, and the best windows for a genuinely enjoyable visit.

Tourism surged after 2020, spring break overwhelmed quiet communities, and curfews followed. Here's what to expect and when to actually visit.

What Changed After 2020

When Florida reopened ahead of most states in spring 2020, 30A absorbed enormous pent-up demand. A simultaneous housing boom — driven by remote work and low mortgage rates — added new inventory and new visitors. The result: a significantly busier, more expensive 30A than existed before the pandemic, with no sign of returning to pre-2020 levels.

Spring Break

30A closes at ~10pm. It was never designed for nightlife. Spring break crowds with nowhere to go resulted in documented scenes of large groups at a closed Seaside after midnight. Communities responded with curfews for minors during spring break.

⚠️ Spring Break on 30A

Spring breakers will have a better experience in Panama City Beach or Destin — both stay open later and have the infrastructure for it. 30A is unambiguously not that place.

Best Times to Visit

📅 Recommended Windows

  • Early May — Warm water, warm weather, dramatically smaller crowds, better restaurant availability
  • Late September / October — Water still warm (upper 70s), shoulder season pricing, excellent weather
  • June — Busy but typically less overwhelming than July/August if summer is your only option
  • Avoid: Mid-March to early April (spring break), July 4th weekend, Labor Day weekend
